Lion

Lions are the second largest cat in the world and the most social of all big cats. Lions live in groups called prides, which consist of about 10 to 15 lions.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera leo.
  • Average Length: 8-10 feet (2.4-3 meters).
  • Average Lifespan: 10-14 years (wild).
  • Habitat: African grasslands and savannas.

African lions are mostly found in sub-Saharan Africa. They used to be found all over Africa but now they have mostly disappeared from North Africa and are at risk of extinction in West Africa. Lions live in savannahs and grasslands, where some cover and plenty of water can be found. African lions once roamed most of Africa and parts of Asia and Europe. But the species has disappeared from 94 percent of its historic range and can only be found today in parts of sub-Saharan Africa.
